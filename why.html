Coding for All

Our world is changing and every child deserves a chance to compete in the technology workforce.

The Need

From 2010-2022 the US expects 1.4 million computing-related job openings, there are only enough American graduates to fill 32% of these jobs.

Source: TechBridge: Changing the Game for Girls in STEM

Growing the Workforce

In order to meet the needs of our growing and changing economy, the technology and computing industry needs to diversify its workforce.

As of 2014, women made up only 12% of the engineering workforce, and 26% of the computing workforce despite being 47% of the overall American workforce.

"At Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Yahoo, Google and eBay, the portion of U.S.-based tech workers who were either black or Hispanic ranged between 3 and 4 percent."

Sources: TechBridge, US Department of Labor, Working Partnerships USA

Code Kitty

Code Kitty uses research-based strategies to support both female and minority students to foster an interest in technology while not excluding other students in the room. The goal is to prepare all students for an opportunity in the future economy.

Bring Code Kitty to Your School

Code Kitty trains teachers to be able to bring robotics and coding into every classroom so every student has an opportunity to learn cutting edge technology skills.
